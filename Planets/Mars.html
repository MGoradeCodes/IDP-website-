Mars: The Red Planet
Fourth planet from the Sun - Mars is the second smallest planet in the solar system.
Red color - Its surface looks red because of iron oxide (rust) in the soil.
Thin atmosphere - Mostly made of carbon dioxide, making it impossible for humans to breathe.
Coldest desert planet - Temperatures can drop to -195Â°F (-125Â°C) at night.

Giant volcano - Olympus Mons is the largest volcano in the solar system, nearly three times taller than Mount Everest.
Deep canyon - Valles Marineris is a canyon system four times deeper and ten times longer than the Grand Canyon.
Strong dust storms - The largest dust storms in the solar system can last for months.
Two small moons - Phobos and Deimos, both are likely captured asteroids.
Evidence of water - Scientists have found signs of frozen water at the poles and underground.
Future human missions - NASA and SpaceX plan to send astronauts to Mars in the coming decades.
