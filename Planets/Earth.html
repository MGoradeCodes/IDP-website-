Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only one known to support life. It is often called the "Blue Planet" because about 71% of its surface is covered in water. The rest of the surface is made up of continents, islands, and mountains. Earth's atmosphere is made mostly of nitrogen and oxygen, which helps living things breathe and protects us from the Sun's harmful rays. The atmosphere also traps heat, keeping the planet warm enough for plants, animals, and humans to survive. Earth's Layers

Earth is made up of four main layers:
Crust - The outermost layer where we live. It's thin compared to the other layers, like the skin of an apple!
Mantle - A hot, thick layer of rock beneath the crust. It slowly moves, which causes earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.
Outer Core - Made of liquid iron and nickel, this layer creates Earth's magnetic field.
Inner Core - A solid metal ball, mostly iron, that is as hot as the Sun!

Earth's Atmosphere
Earth's atmosphere is made of five layers:
Troposphere - Where all weather happens! It's the closest layer to Earth's surface.
Stratosphere - Contains the ozone layer, which protects us from harmful UV rays.
Mesosphere - The coldest layer, where meteors burn up when entering Earth's atmosphere.
Thermosphere - Very hot! This is where the International Space Station (ISS) orbits.
Exosphere - The outermost layer, where Earth's atmosphere fades into space.
