Saturn: The Ringed Giant Second Largest Planet - Saturn is the second biggest planet in the solar system, after Jupiter.
Famous for Its Rings - Saturn has the largest and brightest ring system, made of ice and rock.
Gas Giant - Mostly made of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surface.
Least Dense Planet - Saturn is so light that it could float on water (if there were a big enough ocean!).
Many Moons - Has over 140 moons, including:

Titan - Saturn's biggest moon, with thick clouds and lakes of liquid methane.
Enceladus - An icy moon that shoots water into space and may have an underground ocean.
Fast Rotation - Spins once every 10.7 hours, creating powerful storms and winds.
Strong Magnetic Field - Protects Saturn from solar radiation, like Earth's magnetic field.
Stormy Atmosphere - Features a hexagon-shaped storm at its north pole, unlike anything else in the solar system!
Explored by Cassini - NASA's Cassini spacecraft studied Saturn for 13 years, revealing stunning details about its rings and moons.
Saturn is a beautiful and mysterious planet, with fabulous rings, powerful storms, and moons.
